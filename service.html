Medical Emergency Response Plan (MERP)

Each facility should create a site-specific Medical Emergency Response Plan to handle medical crises (MERP). In addition to detailing the reaction to various medical emergency scenarios based on the health risk and local medical resource evaluations, this should consider the possibility of both single and many casualties and account for the availability of sufficient resources. The MERP should consider any engagement with local health authorities, as well as any unique needs connected to the work activities and the general medical competence available in the nation/location in which these activities are carried out. The capability of the staff, the accessibility of medical supplies, and the adaptability of the plan to handle both major and minor situations all contribute to the success of the Medical Emergency Response (MER) system. To guarantee its effectiveness, frequent drills on various scenarios are necessary. Once the MERP has been created, it should be used frequently and involve testing of the necessary logistical support (such as communications and transport).
